Understanding the Hindu Calendar System

Lunar Calendar System

Purnimanta vs Amanta

Purnimanta: Month ends on Purnima (full moon), used in North India

Amanta: Month ends on Amavasya (new moon), used in South India

Our calendar follows the North Indian Purnimanta system.

Tithi System

Lunar Days (‡§§‡§ø‡§•‡§ø)

A tithi is a lunar day, approximately 1/30th of a lunar month.

  • Shukla Paksha: Waxing moon (1-15 tithis)
  • Krishna Paksha: Waning moon (1-15 tithis)
  • Special Tithis: Ekadashi (11th), Purnima (15th), Amavasya (30th)

Nakshatra System

Lunar Mansions (‡§®‡§ï‡•ç‡§∑‡§§‡•ç‡§∞)

27 nakshatras divide the zodiac, each associated with specific qualities.

Key Nakshatras: Rohini, Mrigashira, Ardra, Pushya, Magha, Uttara Phalguni, Hasta, Swati, Vishakha, Anuradha, Jyeshtha, Mula, Purva Ashadha, Uttara Ashadha, Shravana, Dhanishta, Shatabhisha, Purva Bhadrapada, Uttara Bhadrapada, Revati

Seasonal Cycles

Ritu System (‡§ã‡§§‡•Å)

Hindu calendar recognizes six seasons:

  • Vasant: Spring (Chaitra, Vaishakh)
  • Grishma: Summer (Jyeshtha, Ashadha)
  • Varsha: Monsoon (Shravana, Bhadrapada)
  • Sharad: Autumn (Ashwin, Kartik)
  • Hemant: Pre-winter (Margashirsha, Pausha)
  • Shishir: Winter (Magha, Phalguna)

Panchang Elements

Five Elements (‡§™‡§Ç‡§ö‡§æ‡§Ç‡§ó)

  • Tithi: Lunar day
  • Vara: Day of week
  • Nakshatra: Lunar mansion
  • Yoga: Sun-Moon combination
  • Karana: Half of a tithi

Calendar Usage

Practical Applications

  • Festival Timing: Based on tithi and nakshatra
  • Auspicious Moments: Muhurta for ceremonies
  • Fasting Days: Ekadashi, Purnima, Amavasya
  • Seasonal Practices: Aligned with nature's cycles

Awakening cultural consciousness

Our Mission

To bridge the gap between ancient Hindu wisdom and modern life by providing accessible, accurate calendar information that helps people stay connected with their cultural and spiritual traditions.

Significance

In today's fast-paced world, maintaining connection with cultural roots becomes increasingly important. This application serves as a bridge, making traditional Hindu calendar knowledge accessible to everyone, regardless of their background or location.

Calendar System

We follow the North Indian Purnimanta calendar system, which is based on lunar cycles and is widely used across northern India. This system:

  • Calculates months based on lunar phases
  • Determines festival dates through astronomical calculations
  • Accounts for regional variations in timing
  • Provides accurate tithi (lunar day) information

Note: Dates may vary by 1-2 days based on local astronomical calculations. For important ceremonies, please verify with local authorities.

What We Offer

  • Accurate festival and Ekadashi dates
  • Daily cultural significance and practices
  • Educational content about Hindu months and traditions
  • Modern ways to observe traditional practices
  • Multi-language support (Hindi, Roman, English)
